Candidate for School President

Alex Johnson

Candidate for School President

Alex Johnson is a senior with a proven track record of leadership as former Class President and Debate Team Captain. With a GPA of 4.0 and involvement in over 15 school clubs, Alex understands the diverse needs of our student body. Born and raised in our community, Alex is committed to making our school a more inclusive, engaging, and supportive environment for all students.

Grade: Senior

Major: Political Science & Economics

Experience: 3 years in student government

My Vision for Our School

I believe in a school where every student feels heard, supported, and empowered to achieve their full potential. My presidency will focus on bridging the gap between administration and students, enhancing mental health resources, and creating more opportunities for student involvement in decision-making processes.

Key Agenda Items

  • Establish a Student Mental Health Support Network with trained peer counselors
  • Implement a "Green School Initiative" to reduce waste and promote sustainability
  • Create a transparent budgeting system for student organizations
  • Expand career counseling and internship opportunities for all grades
  • Introduce a monthly "Principal-Student Forum" for direct dialogue
  • Revitalize school spirit through new traditions and events
  • Improve accessibility for students with disabilities across campus
  • Launch a peer tutoring program connecting high-achieving students with those needing academic support

Candidate for Secretary General

Maria Rodriguez

Candidate for Secretary General

Maria Rodriguez is a junior known for her exceptional organizational skills and dedication to student advocacy. As the current Secretary of the Student Activism Club and a contributor to the school newspaper, Maria has demonstrated her ability to manage complex projects while ensuring all voices are heard. Her bilingual abilities (English and Spanish) allow her to connect with a diverse range of students.

Grade: Junior

Major: Communications & Public Relations

Experience: 2 years in student organizations

My Vision for Student Government

As Secretary General, I will ensure transparent communication between the student council, administration, and student body. I believe in documented accountability, accessible information, and creating systems that make student government more efficient and responsive to student needs.

Key Agenda Items

  • Create a publicly accessible digital archive of all council meetings and decisions
  • Implement a weekly email newsletter to keep students informed about council activities
  • Establish a suggestion box system with guaranteed responses within 72 hours
  • Streamline the process for student organizations to request funding
  • Develop a mentorship program for underclassmen interested in student government
  • Create a mobile app for school announcements and event notifications
  • Improve documentation and follow-up on student complaints and suggestions
  • Organize quarterly "State of the School" addresses from the student council

Candidate for Cabinet Secretary for Education

David Chen

Candidate for Cabinet Secretary for Education

David Chen is a senior with a passion for educational equity and innovation. As a National Merit Scholar and founder of the "Students Teaching Students" initiative, David has firsthand experience with both the challenges and opportunities in our educational system. His research on learning styles has been recognized at the state level, and he brings data-driven solutions to educational challenges.

Grade: Senior

Major: Education & Cognitive Science

Experience: Tutoring coordinator, 3 years

My Vision for Academic Excellence

I believe every student can achieve academic success with the right support system. As Education Secretary, I will work to bridge achievement gaps, promote innovative teaching methods, and ensure that all students have access to the resources they need to thrive academically.

Key Agenda Items

  • Expand free peer tutoring to cover all major subjects
  • Advocate for more flexible deadlines and assignment options
  • Create a "Digital Resource Hub" with study materials for all courses
  • Establish regular feedback sessions between students and teachers
  • Implement a "Study Skills Workshop" series for all grade levels
  • Promote inclusive curriculum that reflects diverse perspectives
  • Increase access to advanced courses for underrepresented students
  • Develop a "Finals Support Program" with extended library hours and stress-relief activities
